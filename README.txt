**Bug Repository Read Me**

Welcome to our bug repository! This is the designated space for storing and tracking bugs found within our software. Your contributions here play a vital role in ensuring the quality and stability of our product.

**How to Use:**

1. **Add New Bugs:** To add a new bug to the repository, simply create a new issue using the provided template. Be sure to include all relevant details such as the steps to reproduce, expected behavior, actual behavior, and any error messages encountered.

2. **Assign Bugs:** Once a bug is added, it will be reviewed by the team. A member of the team will then be assigned to investigate and address the issue.

3. **Track Progress:** You can track the progress of bugs by monitoring their status. Bugs may go through stages such as "open," "in progress," "under review," and "resolved."

4. **Provide Updates:** If you have additional information or insights related to a bug, feel free to comment on the respective issue. Your input can help expedite the resolution process.

**Best Practices:**

1. **Be Detailed:** Provide thorough descriptions of bugs to ensure clear understanding by the team. Include screenshots, logs, or any other relevant information that can aid in the bug-fixing process.

2. **Stay Organized:** Use labels and milestones to categorize and prioritize bugs. This helps in managing the bug repository efficiently and ensures that critical issues receive prompt attention.

3. **Collaborate:** Collaboration is key to resolving bugs effectively. Feel free to collaborate with team members, discuss solutions, and offer assistance where possible.

**Security and Confidentiality:**

1. **Sensitive Information:** Avoid including any sensitive or confidential information in bug reports. Stick to describing the technical aspects of the issue without disclosing sensitive data.

2. **Security Concerns:** If you discover a security vulnerability, exercise caution and report it through the appropriate channels. Do not disclose security vulnerabilities publicly in this repository.

Thank you for contributing to the improvement of our software by documenting bugs you encounter. Your efforts help us deliver a more reliable and robust product. If you have any questions or need assistance, don't hesitate to reach out to the team.
